summary: Presented self-supervised deep learning methods for denoising ROFEX tomography sinogram data at HZDR, demonstrating improved reconstruction quality over classical baselines.
abstract: |
  Tomographic imaging enables the reconstruction of cross-sectional views of internal object structures from projection data acquired at multiple angles. However, data from ultrafast electron beam X-ray CT systems such as ROFEX at HZDR is often affected by noise, limiting image quality and reconstruction accuracy. This work investigates self-supervised deep learning denoising methods — including Noise2Void and N2V2 — applied directly to noisy sinograms, without requiring clean ground-truth targets. Compared against classical baselines such as BM3D and temporal averaging, the approach shows improved PSNR and clearer reconstructed tomograms, highlighting the potential of AI-assisted denoising for scientific and industrial tomography workflows.
